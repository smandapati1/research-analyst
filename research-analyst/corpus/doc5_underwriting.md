# Climate Risk Pricing in Commercial Real Estate Underwriting

Commercial real estate lenders and insurers have historically priced climate
risk primarily through physical risk factors: flood zones, wildfire
exposure, and hurricane risk. Transition risk, the financial risk associated
with a shift to a lower-carbon economy, including regulatory penalties,
stranded assets, and shifting tenant demand for low-carbon buildings, has
been priced far less consistently.

Embodied carbon sits at the intersection of both categories. A building with
high embodied carbon is not necessarily at higher physical risk, but it is
more exposed to transition risk from regulations like California AB 2446,
disclosure requirements like Scope 3, and reputational or leasing risk if
tenants and buyers increasingly prefer verified low-carbon buildings.

Lenders who lack embodied carbon data at underwriting are, in effect,
pricing transition risk with an incomplete picture, similar to underwriting
flood risk without flood maps. As regulatory catalysts accumulate, the gap
between lenders with embodied carbon data and those without is expected to
widen, particularly for larger commercial portfolios where a small pricing
error compounds across many assets.

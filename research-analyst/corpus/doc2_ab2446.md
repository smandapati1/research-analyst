# California AB 2446 and Embodied Carbon Disclosure

California Assembly Bill 2446 introduces embodied carbon reduction requirements
for certain building materials used in state-regulated construction projects.
The bill requires the state to develop a maximum acceptable global warming
potential (GWP) for eligible materials, with Environmental Product Declarations
required to demonstrate compliance.

For commercial real estate lenders operating in California, this creates a
near-term compliance obligation: buildings under construction or major
renovation will need documented embodied carbon figures before materials can
be approved for use. Lenders financing these projects will increasingly ask
for embodied carbon documentation as part of due diligence, not just for
regulatory compliance but because it is becoming a proxy for long-term asset
value and climate risk exposure.

This regulatory shift is one of several catalysts pushing embodied carbon
data from a niche sustainability metric toward a standard underwriting input,
similar to how flood zone data became a standard part of property risk
assessment.

# Colorado C-PACE Financing and Embodied Carbon

Commercial Property Assessed Clean Energy (C-PACE) financing in Colorado
allows building owners to finance qualifying energy efficiency, renewable
energy, and increasingly, embodied carbon reduction measures through a
special assessment repaid via the property tax bill. Because C-PACE
financing is tied to the property rather than the borrower, it survives
a sale and is treated similarly to a tax lien for underwriting purposes.

For a building to qualify for C-PACE financing related to embodied carbon
measures, such as low-carbon concrete or mass timber structural systems,
the savings or reduction must generally be quantified and verified,
typically requiring a third-party assessment. This has created early demand
in Colorado for standardized embodied carbon measurement and verification
services, particularly from lenders who need to confirm that the projected
carbon reduction (and associated risk reduction) justifies the financing
terms.

C-PACE embodied carbon financing remains a small but fast-growing niche
compared to traditional energy efficiency C-PACE deals, which have
historically dominated the program nationally.

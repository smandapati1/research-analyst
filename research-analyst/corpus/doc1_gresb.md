# GRESB Asset-Level Scoring (2026)

GRESB (Global Real Estate Sustainability Benchmark) is shifting toward asset-level
scoring starting in 2026, moving away from purely portfolio-level ESG assessments.
This means individual buildings, not just fund-level aggregates, will receive
sustainability scores that factor in embodied carbon, operational emissions, and
climate risk exposure.

Institutional lenders and insurers who rely on GRESB data for underwriting will need
asset-level embodied carbon figures to feed into their scoring models. Historically,
embodied carbon data (the emissions locked into a building's materials and
construction process) has been difficult to obtain at the individual asset level,
since most Environmental Product Declarations (EPDs) are aggregated at the
manufacturer or product-category level rather than tied to a specific building.

The shift to asset-level scoring creates demand for tools that can estimate or
verify a specific building's embodied carbon footprint at the point of sale,
refinance, or insurance underwriting, rather than relying on portfolio-wide
averages.

# Scope 3 Emissions Requirements Arriving 2027

Scope 3 emissions, the indirect emissions that occur across a company's value
chain, are set to become a mandatory disclosure requirement for many large
companies starting in 2027 under evolving SEC and state-level climate
disclosure rules. For real estate investment trusts and commercial property
owners, embodied carbon in building materials falls squarely within Scope 3
reporting, since it originates from suppliers and construction activity
rather than the building's own operations.

This creates a compliance deadline that is different from operational carbon
reporting, which most large real estate portfolios already track through
utility data. Embodied carbon has no equivalent utility bill; it must be
calculated at the point of construction or estimated retroactively using
materials records, which are often incomplete or scattered across multiple
contractors.

Companies preparing for 2027 Scope 3 requirements are expected to begin
building internal data infrastructure for embodied carbon tracking well
before the deadline, creating a multi-year window where third-party data and
scoring tools have a clear buyer.
